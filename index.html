Role Manual

Director of Marketing

This manual defines what it means to lead marketing at an EOS Hospitality property. Whether you are joining the team for the first time or you have been with us for years, this is the shared standard we hold ourselves to.

Marketing at EOS is both strategic and hands-on. You are the person connecting brand vision to daily execution, translating data into direction, and making sure every guest touchpoint reflects who we are as a property and as a company.

Use this manual as your operating framework. Come back to it when priorities shift, when you are onboarding a new team member, or when you want to gut-check that nothing is slipping through the cracks. The expectations here are not aspirational. They are the baseline for how we run marketing across the portfolio.

C
Culture

Before anything else, every member of the commercial team should be grounded in who we are. Our culture is not a poster on the wall. It is the lens through which we make decisions, interact with guests, and represent our properties.

EOS Culture Card2 items
Keep a copy of the EOS ETHOS on your person while on property.
This is not symbolic. It is a reference point for how we operate and what we stand for. If someone asks you what EOS is about, you should be able to answer without hesitation.
Be able to explain the relevance of at least two of our positioning statements.
You do not need to recite them word for word, but you should understand what they mean and how they connect to the work your team does every day.
Why This Matters

Culture is the foundation of brand. If the marketing team does not understand and embody the brand, no campaign or creative asset will make up for it. Every piece of content you produce, every vendor you brief, and every guest interaction your property delivers should be informed by these principles.

D
Daily Responsibilities

Your daily rhythm is the engine of your marketing operation. These activities should feel like second nature. If you are new to the role, focus here first. Master the daily rhythm before layering in everything else.

Marketing Orchestration5 items
▾
Monitor work progress, provide feedback, and troubleshoot issues.
Check in on active projects and campaigns. Know where things stand before someone has to tell you. If something is stalled, your job is to unstick it.
Review and approve marketing materials.
Nothing goes out the door without your eyes on it. This includes social posts, email campaigns, website updates, printed collateral, and any guest-facing communications. You are the quality filter.
Coordinate with internal and external resources on project tasks and campaign tactics.
You are working with your on-property team, corporate marketing, agency partners, and vendors daily. Make sure everyone knows what they are responsible for and when it is due.
Triage and assign new requests, resolve tickets.
Requests come in from the GM, sales, F&B, ownership, and corporate. Your job is to evaluate, prioritize, and route them. Not everything is urgent. Part of your value is knowing the difference.
Develop creative briefs, channel plans, and maintain project trackers.
Good marketing starts with a clear brief. Every campaign, initiative, or request should have a documented scope before work begins.
Strategic Oversight6 items
▾
Review overall hotel performance and coordinate with revenue and sales on urgent items.
You do not operate in a silo. Check pickup, pace, and any revenue alerts. If there is a need date or a soft period, marketing should be part of the response.
Monitor active campaigns and performance metrics to ensure KPIs are tracking toward goals.
If you have paid media, email, or social campaigns running, check performance daily. Do not wait for the monthly report to find out something is underperforming.
Track real-time trends and potential brand risks across media and social platforms.
Know what is being said about your property. Monitor reviews, social mentions, and any press coverage. If something negative is gaining traction, flag it early.
Analyze qualitative data and sentiment on earned and owned channels.
Go beyond the numbers. Read the actual reviews, comments, and guest feedback. Patterns in sentiment often reveal opportunities that metrics alone will miss.
Review platform, partner, and technology changes and assess their impact.
The platforms we use change constantly. Stay current on updates that affect how we reach guests. Share relevant changes with your team and agency partners.
Develop actionable insights and communicate marketing direction based on data.
Your job is not just to collect data. It is to interpret it and tell the team what to do about it. A good Director of Marketing turns numbers into direction.
Tactical Marketing5 items
▾
Implement plans and build deliverables.
Research, writing, editing, design, and production. Some of this you will do yourself. Some you will manage through your team or agency partners. Either way, you own the output.
Vet influencers, media, partners, and inbound marketing opportunities.
Not every opportunity is worth pursuing. Evaluate fit, audience, reach, and cost before committing. Protect the brand and the budget.
Manage the logistics of events, activations, partnerships, photoshoots, and media visits.
Marketing often involves coordination-heavy projects. Own the logistics so the creative work can shine.
Launch campaigns, update systems, and publish marketing materials across channels.
When it is go time, make sure everything is live, correct, and functioning. Double-check links, tracking codes, landing pages, and creative assets.
Double-check work as it launches for quality control and resolve issues.
Mistakes happen. The best marketers catch them fast. Review everything in its live environment after launch, not just in the preview.
W
Weekly Responsibilities

Weekly touchpoints keep your team aligned, your stakeholders informed, and your projects on track. The daily work keeps the engine running. The weekly work makes sure the engine is pointed in the right direction.

Coordination and Meetings7 items
▾
Brief your marketing team on priorities, deliverables, and deadlines.
Start each week with clarity. Your team should know what matters most this week, what is due, and what is coming next.
Conduct 1:1s with direct reports and internal marketing meetings.
Regular 1:1s are non-negotiable. This is where you coach, unblock, and develop your team. Do not cancel these for other meetings.
Coordinate external meetings with agencies, vendors, and brand partners.
Your paid media agency, PR team, social partners, and other external resources need regular touchpoints. Weekly check-ins keep deliverables on track.
Attend commercial meetings to align messaging and promotions.
Marketing does not set strategy in isolation. Show up to the commercial meetings, understand what revenue and sales need, and make sure your campaigns support the broader goals.
Lead critical path calls for renovations, relaunches, and special initiatives.
If your property has a renovation, rebrand, or major launch coming, you should be leading or actively participating in the planning calls.
Meet with property stakeholders across departments for marketing planning.
F&B, spa, front desk, activities. These teams create the experiences you are marketing. Build relationships and keep the communication loop open.
Share marketing performance updates and upcoming initiatives.
Do not wait to be asked. Proactively share wins, learnings, and what is coming. A brief weekly update to the GM and key stakeholders goes a long way.
M
Monthly Responsibilities

Monthly is where you shift from execution to evaluation. This is the cadence where you step back, look at what happened, measure it, manage your budget, and prepare for what is ahead.

Project Management2 items
▾
Close out completed projects, archive assets, and finalize documents for case studies.
When a campaign or project wraps, close the loop. Archive the final assets, document what worked and what did not.
Coordinate with property teams to ensure timely delivery of marketing inputs.
Photography, menu updates, event details, room renovations. Marketing depends on timely inputs from other departments. Own the follow-up.
Performance and Reporting3 items
▾
Review digital marketing KPIs and evaluate channel performance.
This is your monthly health check. Look at the full picture across site, email, social, OTA, and paid ads.
Review press reports from PR and collect data on activations and field marketing.
Earned media is part of the marketing mix. Collect and consolidate what your PR team delivered.
Prepare monthly reporting and present to stakeholders.
Your monthly report is how leadership understands the value marketing is delivering. Lead with insights, not just data.
Budget Management4 items
▾
Review, code, and track invoices to ensure timely vendor payment.
Vendor relationships depend on timely payment. Code invoices correctly and submit them on schedule.
Review forecasted expenses against actuals and advise finance of accruals or updates.
Know where your budget stands at all times. Communicate proactively to finance.
Compare actual paid spend vs. monthly budget allocation.
Paid media budgets can drift if not monitored. Check actual spend against plan monthly.
Reallocate budget based on campaign performance.
If something is working, invest more. If something is underperforming, pull back and redirect.
Tactical Marketing3 items
▾
Implement campaign changes and direction based on performance.
Use insights from your monthly review to make adjustments.
Prepare materials for upcoming events, campaigns, and seasonal refreshes.
Always be working one month ahead. If you are only reacting to what is in front of you, you are already behind.
Audit digital channels and make updates.
Walk through your website, OTA listings, Google Business Profile, social profiles, and email templates monthly.
Q
Quarterly Responsibilities

Quarterly is where you zoom out. The daily, weekly, and monthly rhythms keep the operation running. The quarterly cadence is where you evaluate whether the operation is driving the right results.

Strategic Review3 items
▾
Evaluate quarterly marketing performance vs. revenue and occupancy goals.
Connect your KPIs to the outcomes that matter: revenue, occupancy, ADR, and direct booking share.
Plan next quarter's campaigns and activations.
Build your campaign plan based on what you learned this quarter and what the commercial team is targeting next.
Refine marketing plan and focus areas.
Your annual marketing plan is not static. Each quarter is an opportunity to adjust focus.
Tactical Marketing3 items
▾
Update core guest communications: policy pages, hours of operation, and lifecycle touchpoints.
These are the details guests rely on. Make sure every touchpoint reflects reality.
Refresh seasonal creative across channels.
Update hero imagery, social content themes, email templates, and website banners. Stale creative signals a stale property.
Ensure on-property materials, sales items, and menus are updated and on brand.
Walk the property. Check lobby collateral, in-room materials, restaurant menus, spa menus, and any printed pieces.
A
Annual Responsibilities

Annual planning is where strategy meets execution. This is the work that shapes the entire year ahead for your property's marketing program.

Marketing Budget and Business Plan3 items
▾
Develop the annual marketing budget and calendar aligned with hotel business goals.
Build it from the goals down: what are we trying to achieve, and what does it cost to get there?
Gather proposals and plans from vendors and partners, negotiate pricing.
Give yourself enough lead time to evaluate options and negotiate effectively. Do not auto-renew without reviewing performance.
Present the marketing plan to ownership or executive committee.
Tell a clear story that connects marketing investment to business outcomes.
Brand and Market Review3 items
▾
Conduct a full brand audit covering messaging, visuals, and digital presence.
Once a year, take a hard look at everything. Is the brand consistent across every channel?
Benchmark performance against competitor and brand reports.
Pull comp set data, review STR reports, and benchmark your digital performance against competitors.
Identify new market opportunities or traveler segments.
Markets evolve. Use your annual review to look beyond your current audience.
Strategy and Positioning4 items
▾
Define annual campaigns to strengthen brand awareness and direct bookings.
Your biggest campaigns should be planned annually, not scrambled at the last minute.
Align marketing objectives with revenue, loyalty, and guest satisfaction targets.
If revenue is focused on midweek occupancy, marketing should be driving midweek demand.
Review performance of all partners, vendors, and marketing channels.
Evaluate each one annually. Are they delivering value? Are the economics still working?
Develop plans for relaunches, rebrands, and major operational changes.
If a renovation or rebrand is on the horizon, annual planning is when you scope the marketing workstream.
Training and Team Development2 items
▾
Conduct annual performance reviews and set KPIs for your marketing team.
Set clear expectations, provide honest feedback, and define measurable KPIs.
Provide training on marketing systems, technical skills, and professional development.
The tools and platforms we use evolve constantly. Make sure your team has the skills to keep up.
Sustainability and CSR Marketing2 items
▾
Promote the hotel's sustainability initiatives and community engagement programs.
Make sure your property's efforts are visible and authentic in marketing communications.
Ensure CSR messaging is authentic and visible in marketing communications.
Do not greenwash. If your property has genuine sustainability practices, tell that story.
